Burger Recipe

Description

Burgers are a timeless favorite, known for their juicy patties, melted cheese, and endless topping options. Whether grilled or pan-fried, a well-made burger is a satisfying meal that’s quick, customizable, and always delicious.

This recipe walks you through making classic homemade burgers from scratch, including how to cook the perfect patty and build your burger just the way you like it.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb (450g) ground beef (80% lean)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 4 burger buns
  • 4 slices of cheese (optional)
  • Lettuce leaves
  • Tomato slices
  • Onion slices (red or white)
  • Pickles (optional)
  • Ketchup, mustard, or mayonnaise (as desired)
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil or butter (for toasting buns)

Instructions

  1. Divide the ground beef into 4 equal portions and gently shape into patties, about ¾ inch thick. Don’t overwork the meat.
  2. Season both sides of each patty with salt and pepper just before cooking.
  3. Preheat a skillet or grill over medium-high heat. Lightly oil the surface if using a skillet.
  4. Cook the patties for about 3–4 minutes per side, or until they reach your preferred level of doneness. Add cheese during the last minute if desired, and cover to melt.
  5. While the burgers cook, toast the buns cut-side-down in a pan or on the grill for 1–2 minutes until golden brown.
  6. Assemble the burgers: place the patty on the bottom bun, then add lettuce, tomato, onion, pickles, and condiments of your choice. Top with the other half of the bun.
  7. Serve immediately and enjoy your homemade burgers!
